The Value of Body Cameras and Documented Reporting

May 2026 · 3 min read

Security is only as good as what you can prove. A patrol that is not documented might as well not have happened — and an incident without a record is a liability. That is why body cameras and time-stamped reporting have become non-negotiable in modern private security.

Why body cameras matter

Axon body cameras capture what happens on scene, from arrival to resolution. That footage protects everyone: it provides evidence when incidents occur, keeps interactions professional, and gives property owners an objective record rather than a he-said-she-said.

Reports you can rely on

Every patrol should end with a time-stamped, photo-documented report. That turns a vague “we came by” into a provable record of when officers arrived, what they found, and what they did. For boards and property managers, it is the difference between hoping the work happened and knowing it did.

Protection for property and officers alike

Documentation cuts both ways in the best sense — it protects your property’s interests and your security team’s integrity. When a claim or dispute arises, the evidence is already there.

Every RCJ Protection Agency officer wears a body camera, and every visit is logged and documented. It is part of how we deliver accountability, not just presence.
